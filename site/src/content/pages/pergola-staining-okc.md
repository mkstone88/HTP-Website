## Frequently Asked Questions: Pergola Staining OKC

<details class="faq-item"><summary>How much does pergola staining cost?</summary><div><p>A lot of factors affect the cost — the condition of the wood and how much masking is needed both play a role. In our area, staining a pergola usually falls between $1,500 and $5,000. The best way to know for sure is a free estimate.</p></div></details>
<details class="faq-item"><summary>How long will the pergola stain last?</summary><div><p>Wood Defender recommends staining every 2–4 years, so you restain before major fading or graying. Semi-transparent stains often last a year or more longer than transparent stains.</p></div></details>
<details class="faq-item"><summary>How long does it take to stain a pergola?</summary><div><p>We can stain most pergolas in one day. If the wood needs to be washed first, we’ll wait 24–48 hours for it to dry.</p></div></details>
<details class="faq-item"><summary>What should I do to prepare for stain day?</summary><div><p>Move any decor or landscaping away from the pergola, and turn off sprinklers 24 hours ahead so water doesn’t get on the wood.</p></div></details>

## Our Pergola Staining Process

Oklahoma City’s weather can be hard on a pergola, deck, or fence. To make sure our OKC homeowners get the best results, we follow a proven process — developed from specialized training with Wood Defender, one of America’s leading stain manufacturers.

1. **Initial consultation** — We start by listening to you: the look you want, the questions you have, and how we can help protect your outdoor space.
2. **Moisture Check** — Dry wood is critical to good stain work, so we use a moisture meter to measure the wood’s actual moisture content.
3. **Wind Check** — Wind is always a concern in OKC. We take precautions for where it may carry the stain as we apply it.
4. **Wood Prep** — We brush away any dirt or dry grass stuck to the wood.
5. **Mix Stain** — Thoroughly mixing the stain before applying is key to an even, uniform finish.
6. **Mask Surrounding Areas** — We cover any plants, patios, or furniture near the area we’re staining.
7. **Stain Application** — We spray with an airless sprayer to reach every nook, applying enough to fully saturate and protect the wood.
8. **Cut In** — Areas next to your house or other structures are stained by hand for protection.
9. **Touch Up** — We check the whole pergola to make sure no spots were missed.
10. **Clean Up** — We leave your property as clean as we found it.
